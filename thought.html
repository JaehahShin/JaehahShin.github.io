Definition of Human’s Intuition In Pens´ees, Pascal writes, “Contraries. - Man is naturally credulous and incredulous, timid and rash.” This shows that humans exhibit contradictions depending on their situation. People naturally tend to make decisions that can benefit them regardless of whether they did it purposely or not. Those decisions are usually based on their prior experiences which help them to build up their unique intuitions throughout their life journey. Those are often subconscious. Interactions, cultural exposure, and everything that we face all shapes their unique intuitions, both directly and indirectly. Interestingly enough, people can decide what intuition they would like to build throughout their lives. Intuition subconsciously affects people’s decision-making, and this generally makes it difficult to predict one’s next movement in certain circumstances.
